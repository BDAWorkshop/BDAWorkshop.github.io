Call for papers

Call for papers

Cryptocurrency transactions are probably the most complex and evolving networks publicly accessible. The increasing number of transactions and addresses present a number of challenges to researchers and practitioners, such as data management, transaction tracing, de-anonymization, collective behavior modeling, tokenomics, and economic model development. These challenges call for innovative solutions that leverage machine learning, mathematical modeling, statistical methods, and other approaches to address cryptocurrency transactions' complexities and scalability issues.

Themes (not limited to):

  • Data Management: The massive amount of data generated by cryptocurrency transactions requires effective storage and retrieval mechanisms. Research papers in this area can focus on innovative methods for data storage, indexing, and retrieval and techniques for real-time data processing. We particularly look for papers that can address the issues of cross-chain data fusion.
  • Transaction Tracing: Cryptocurrency transactions are often used for illegal activities such as money laundering and terrorism financing. Papers on this theme can explore techniques for identifying suspicious transactions, tracking the flow of funds, and identifying the individuals or entities involved. We are particularly curious about the probability and transaction tracing methods in zero-knowledge (ZK) protocols.
  • De-anonymization and profiling: Cryptocurrencies are often used for their anonymity features, which can benefit privacy and security. However, they can also be exploited for illicit activities. Papers on this theme can investigate techniques for anonymization and de-anonymization of transactions and approaches for enhancing the privacy and security of cryptocurrency transactions. Transaction patterns can also be used to profile addresses for business purposes.
  • Collective Behavior Modeling: Cryptocurrencies are unique because their value is largely derived from user adoption and collective behavior. Papers on this theme can explore approaches for modeling user behavior, understanding network effects, and analyzing tokenomics. Probable user communities for consideration include DeFi protocol users, blockchain game players, and other web3 application adopters.
  • Tokenomics and economic models: Tokenomics refers to the economic model of a cryptocurrency or token in blockchain network maintenance, DeFi activities, and web3 applications. Papers on this theme can explore mathematical modeling of token distribution, token supply, token utility, token governance, and token market dynamics. We particularly look for papers targeting complex tokenomic scenarios such as DeFi protocols and GameFi.

Important Dates:

  • September 3, 2023: Workshop papers submission
  • September 24, 2023: Notification of workshop papers acceptance to authors
  • October 1, 2023: Camera-ready deadline and copyright form
  • December 4, 2023: Workshops date

Submission guidelines:

  • Paper submissions should be limited to a maximum of 8 pages (plus 2 extra pages), in the IEEE 2-column format.
  • Papers that are not accepted by the main conference will be automatically sent to a workshop selected by the authors when the papers were submitted to the main conference. Please select Blockchain Data Analytics (BDA2023) if you like our topic.
  • All accepted workshop papers will be published in the dedicated ICDMW proceedings published by the IEEE Computer Society Press.
